Mahatma Gandhi

The guru of nonviolent resistance and civil rights movements across the world

Artwork of Mahatma Gandhi

He is recognized as one of the greatest spiritual and politics leaders around 20th century. Honored in India as the father of the nation, pioneer and promotor of the Satyagraha (The resistance to the tirany through massive nonviolent civil disobedience).

He stood for poverty, women rights, religious armony and ethnic. He applied in a supreme way the principles of nonviolent civil disobedience to free his country from foreign domain. He was imprisoned many times due to his actions. He achieved his goal in 1947 when India got his independence from Great Britain.

Due to his greatness he is called Mahatma which means "great spirit". Later he inspired great civil rights leaders from Martin Luther King Jr. to Nelson Mandela on their journey of achieving equal rights for people.
